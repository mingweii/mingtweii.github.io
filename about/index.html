Career as a Physicist

From August 2013, I started my PhD study in experimental condensed matter physics at Duke University and join Prof. Gleb Finkelstein's group. In my first two years as an infant physicist, I had worked on the fabrication of chrominm tunneling junctions, silicon germanium nanowires, carbon nanotubes, and CVD graphene but they did not turn out well. Thanks to the new discovery of supercurrents flowing along the quantum Hall edge states in a ballistic graphene Josephson junction done by my colleagues, François Amet et al., which was published in Science in 2016, I was furtunate to switch to this new and exciting field and continue investigating the transport mechanisms of quantum Hall supercurrents by several approaches. This leads to my dissertation.

In September 2018, I joined Prof. James Williams' group in Joint Quantum Institute, University of Maryland at College Park, as a postdoctoral researcher.
