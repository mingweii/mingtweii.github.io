Welcome to my personal website!

I'm an experimental quantum physicist with a background of electrical engineering. I'm enthusiastic about learning and developing innovative technologies and solving important problems.

I received my Ph.D. degree in Physics from Duke University in Finkelstein Group. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in Quantum Coherent Device Physics Group at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory.

During my Ph.D. and postdoc trainings in physics, I have thus far contributed 12 peer-reviewed scientific publications on superconducting quantum devices. In the meanwhile, I developed proficient knowledge in solid state physics and quantum physics, and also acquired diverse expertises in superconducting device fabrication and characterization, dilution refrigeration, low-noise electronics, measurement and instrumentation with Python and LabVIEW, 3D CAD design and fast prototyping with 3D printing, RF / microwave noise measurement, electromagnetic simulation with COMSOL Multiphysics and ANSYS HFSS, and data analysis / numerical modeling with Python and MATLAB.

Prior to my Ph.D. study in physics, I obtained a B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, then worked in the display industry (AU Optronics and Qualcomm MEMS Technology) for about a year, performing OLED fabrication development and characterization, failure analysis on controller boards, and PCB schematics design for a multi-board testing platform.
