Welcome to Ming's personal website!

I am an experimental condensed matter physicist specializing in quantum transport measurements and nanofabrication of superconducting devices made of quantum materials. I'm excited about not only exploring novel quantum phenomena but also learning the latest developments in quantum computing industry. Feel free to contact me if you want to discuss new projects with me!

I received my Ph.D. degree in Physics from Duke University in Finkelstein Group, where I have extensively studied a variety of transport phenomena in ballistic graphene Josephson junctions, especially at high magnetic fields in the integer quantum Hall regime. I am currently working as a postdoctoral researcher in Williams Group at the University of Maryland-College Park. My recent research interest focuses on:

  • DC/AC/RF transport measurements of unusual Josephson effects in low-dimensional quantum materials, such as ferroelectric SnTe nanowires and 2D van der Waals materials like ferromagnets Fe3GeTe2 and Cr2Ge2Te6.
  • Developing shot noise and cross-correlation measurement setups at hundreds of MHz to study the quasiparticle statistics at the interface of topological phases of matter and superconductors.
