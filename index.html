Welcome to my personal website!

I am both a hardware engineer and a physicist enthusiastic about innovative technologies and solving important problems. I recently joined Qualcomm Atheros in Santa Clara, CA, USA, as a senior engineer in September 2022. My primary responsibility is to develop automated testing for latest Wi-Fi RF ICs. I am excited about adapting my experience in experimental physics to my new career in the wireless communication industry.

Before joining the industry, I was trained as an experimental quantum physicist for nine years at Duke University, University of Maryland, and Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, where I had contributed 12 peer-reviewed publications on superconducting quantum devices, developed proficient knowledge in solid state physics and quantum physics, and also acquired diverse hands-on skills in superconducting device fabrication and characterization, dilution refrigeration, low-noise electronics, measurement and instrumentation with Python and LabVIEW, 3D CAD mechanical design and prototyping, RF noise measurement, electromagnetic simulation with COMSOL Multiphysics and ANSYS HFSS, and data analysis / numerical modeling with Python and MATLAB.

Prior to my Ph.D. study in physics, I obtained a B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, then worked in the display industry (AU Optronics and Qualcomm MEMS Technology) for about a year, performing OLED fabrication development and characterization, failure analysis on controller boards, and PCB schematics design for a multi-board testing platform.
