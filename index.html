Welcome to my personal website!

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in Williams Group at the University of Maryland-College Park. My recent research interest focuses on:

  • DC/AC/RF transport measurements of unusual Josephson effects in low-dimensional quantum materials, such as two-band Josephson effects in ferroelectric SnTe nanowires and 2D ferromagnetic Fe3GeTe2.
  • Developing shot noise and cross-correlation measurement setups at hundreds of MHz to study the quasiparticle statistics at the interface of topological phases of matter and superconductors.

I received my Ph.D. degree in Physics from Duke University in Finkelstein Group, where I have extensively studied a variety of transport phenomena in ballistic graphene Josephson junctions:

  • Temperature activation in long and short junction regimes, anomalous magnetic diffraction patterns, Shapiro steps and multiterminal supercurrent flows.
  • Observation of supercurrents and enhanced conductance due to tunneling of chiral Andreev edge states in a short constricted channel.
  • Manipulation of edge-flowing supercurrents in the quantum Hall regime by top- or side-gating the vacuum edges, and also by injecting a normal current in a three-terminal device.
