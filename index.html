Welcome to my personal website!

I'm an experienced quantum physicist enthusiastic about learning and developing innovative technology and solving important problems.

I received my Ph.D. degree in Physics from Duke University in Finkelstein Group. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in Quantum Coherent Device Physics Group at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory.

During my Ph.D. and postdoc training in physics, I have thus far contributed to the scientific community with 12 peer-reviewed scientific journals on superconducting quantum devices since 2016. In the meanwhile, I developed proficient knowledge in quantum electronics and solid state physics, and also acquired diverse expertise in superconducting device fabrication and characterization, dilution refrigeration, low-noise electronics, measurement and instrumentation with Python and LabVIEW, 3D CAD design and 3D printing prototyping, RF / microwave noise measurement, RF / microwave simulation with COMSOL Multiphysics and HFSS, and data analysis/modeling with Python and MATLAB.

Prior to my Ph.D. study in physics, I obtained a B.S. degree in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, then worked in the display Industry (AU Optronics and Qualcomm MEMS Technology) for about a year, performing OLED fabrication development and characterization, failure analysis on controller boards, and PCB schematics design for the multi-board testing platform.
